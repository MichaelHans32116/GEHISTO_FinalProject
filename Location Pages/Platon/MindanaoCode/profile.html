Colonel Wendell Fertig
December 16, 1900 – March 24, 1975

An American officer who led guerrilla forces in Mindanao during WWII. After escaping Japanese capture in 1942, he organized thousands of Filipino fighters, set up a guerrilla government, and conducted sabotage, intelligence, and support missions for the Allies—playing a key role in the island’s liberation.

During World War II, Colonel Wendell Fertig led a major guerrilla movement in Mindanao after the Japanese occupation of the Philippines. Refusing to surrender, Fertig escaped into the mountains and organized a resistance force made up of Filipino fighters, local tribes, and surviving American soldiers. To unify the scattered groups, he declared himself a general and established a makeshift government, even printing guerrilla currency. His forces carried out sabotage missions, gathered intelligence, protected civilians, and disrupted Japanese operations across the island. Fertig also maintained secret radio communications with General Douglas MacArthur, providing valuable information that helped the Allied forces during their return to the Philippines. His leadership kept the spirit of resistance alive and played a vital role in weakening the Japanese hold on Mindanao.
