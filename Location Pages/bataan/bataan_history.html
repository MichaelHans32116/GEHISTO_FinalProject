Bataan Peninsula

The Fall of Bataan and the Infamous Death March (1942)

Key Events

The Japanese Invasion

December 8, 1941 - Initial attacks on Philippine bases

The Japanese attack began just hours after Pearl Harbor, catching American and Filipino forces by surprise. Air raids destroyed most of the US aircraft on the ground.

Battle of Bataan

January 7 - April 9, 1942

American and Filipino forces held out for three months against overwhelming Japanese forces despite severe shortages of food, medicine, and ammunition.

Surrender

April 9, 1942

With his forces starving and out of ammunition, General Edward King surrendered to prevent further loss of life, beginning the infamous Death March.

Survivor Accounts

"The march was hell on earth. The Japanese showed no mercy. If you fell, you were left to die or bayoneted on the spot..."

"...The heat was unbearable and we had no water. Many of my comrades didn't survive those first few days. I still have nightmares about it."

- Lt. John Smith, US Army

"At Camp O'Donnell, death was a daily occurrence. Men died from dysentery, malaria, and starvation..."

"...We buried dozens every morning before roll call. The Japanese provided no medicine and almost no food. It was a slow death for many."

- Cpl. Robert Johnson, US Army Air Corps
