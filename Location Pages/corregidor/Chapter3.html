CHAPTER III
Liberation Landings 1945

The Liberation
of Corregidor

On February 16, 1945, American forces launched Operation Topside, beginning the liberation of Corregidor from Japanese control.

February 16, 1945

Operation Topside Begins

American paratroopers of the 503rd Parachute Infantry Regiment drop onto the Topside plateau of Corregidor, initiating the assault to reclaim the island. Despite strong winds and rugged terrain, the airborne troops manage to secure critical high ground, catching the Japanese defenders by surprise.

February 17, 1945

Beach Landings

Amphibious forces from the 34th Infantry Regiment land at San Jose beach on the southeastern shore. The joint operation between airborne and seaborne units begins to squeeze Japanese positions from multiple directions, establishing a firm Allied foothold on the island.

February 26, 1945

Final Victory

After ten days of brutal combat, including cave-to-cave fighting and massive underground explosions set off by trapped Japanese forces, Corregidor is officially secured. The island's liberation reopens Manila Bay to Allied naval operations and delivers a symbolic blow to Japanese control in the Philippines.
