Japanese landing at Corregidor, 1942

Following the fall of Bataan on April 9, 1942, Corregidor became the final stronghold of Allied forces in the Philippines. On the night of May 5, Japanese forces initiated their assault, landing between North Point and Cavalry Point. Despite fierce resistance from American and Filipino defenders, the Japanese established a beachhead.

By May 6, with Japanese tanks advancing and defensive positions compromised, Lieutenant General Jonathan Wainwright ordered the surrender of Corregidor, marking a significant victory for Japan in the Pacific theater.
