Corregidor Island

The Fall and Recapture

Historical Timeline

The Fall of Corregidor (May 1942)

After the fall of Bataan, Corregidor was the last stronghold of the Allied forces in the Philippines. Despite a valiant defense, the island surrendered to Japanese forces on May 6, 1942, marking a dark chapter in the Pacific War.

Japanese Occupation

Following its capture, the Japanese fortified Corregidor to secure control of Manila Bay. The tunnels and fortifications were repurposed for the Japanese Imperial Army's strategic operations in the Philippines.

Recapture of Corregidor (February 1945)

In a daring combined assault of paratroopers and amphibious forces, U.S. troops retook Corregidor in February 1945. This victory reopened Manila Bay to Allied shipping and marked a symbolic reversal of Japan's earlier triumph.
