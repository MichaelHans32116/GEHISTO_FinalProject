Japanese Landings
(December 1941)

+

On December 22, 1941, the Japanese 14th Army, under Lieutenant General Masaharu Homma, initiated landings at multiple points along the eastern shore of Lingayen Gulf, including San Fernando, Bauang, Caba, Aringay, Agoo, and Santo Tomas.

> Show Info

Occupation and Strategic Importance

+

Following the successful landings, Japanese forces advanced inland, capturing key locations and eventually leading to the fall of Manila. Lingayen Gulf remained under Japanese control for over three years, serving as a vital logistical hub for their operations in the Philippines.

> Show Info

Liberation Landings
(January 1945)

+

On January 9, 1945, the U.S. 6th Army launched a massive amphibious assault to retake Lingayen Gulf. Approximately 68,000 troops landed on the first day, with the total number reaching over 200,000 in subsequent days.
